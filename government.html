What is Government

Government is the system or group of people responsible for making and enforcing laws within a society. It organizes how a country is run, protects the rights of citizens, and provides services such as security, education, and infrastructure. Governments create policies and rules that help maintain order and guide how people interact with each other within a nation.

There are different types of governments, including democracies, monarchies, and authoritarian systems. Each system distributes power differently and determines how leaders are chosen and how decisions are made. Studying government helps people understand political systems, civic responsibilities, and how citizens can participate in shaping their communities and their country's future.

Learning about government is important because it helps citizens become informed about their rights and responsibilities. It also encourages people to participate in civic activities such as voting, community engagement, and public discussions. Understanding government allows individuals to better evaluate policies, leaders, and political decisions that affect society.
